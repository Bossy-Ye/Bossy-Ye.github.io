Research Program

Boshuai Ye

PhD Researcher in Reliable Quantum Workflows and Empirical Validation
University of Oulu

My research focuses on trustworthy quantum software workflows across the classical-to-quantum bridge and heterogeneous computing infrastructures. I study how compilation and mapping effects, implementation variability, execution uncertainty, and resource constraints shape workflow behavior, empirical reliability, and the validity of software-level interpretations. By combining empirical software engineering, validation-centric design, and systems-level analysis, I aim to make quantum software more auditable, reproducible, and technically robust under realistic execution conditions.

Research Themes

Trustworthy Workflow Interpretation

Studying how compilation and mapping effects, implementation variability, and execution uncertainty shape workflow behavior and the validity of software-level interpretations.

Reliable Execution Across Heterogeneous Infrastructures

Designing and evaluating hybrid quantum workflows under resource constraints, scheduling effects, and heterogeneous execution conditions spanning classical and quantum components.

Auditable Empirical Validation

Building executable, auditable research artifacts with pinned environments, repeatable runs, and evidence surfaces that support empirical reliability, independent verification, and re-validation.

My research program is organized around three connected questions: how workflow behavior should be interpreted under variability, how execution remains reliable across heterogeneous infrastructures, and how evidence can be made reproducible and auditable.

Selected Contributions

  • Validation-centric analysis of how workflow behavior and conclusions shift under admissible variability.
  • Reliable hybrid quantum workflows across the classical-to-quantum bridge and heterogeneous execution settings.
  • Reproducible research artifacts and evidence surfaces for auditable empirical studies.

BARC

A systems-oriented study of execution slowdown under bounded resource delivery, using structural predictors, backlog-aware metrics, and lower-bound analysis to explain when static depth misleads executable performance.
