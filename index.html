I study trustworthy quantum software and rigorous evidence for quantum claims, with a focus on reproducibility science, empirical software engineering methods, and practical reliability in quantum systems.

Research Themes

Trustworthy Quantum Software

Designing methods and tools that improve reliability, testability, and maintainability in quantum software lifecycles.

Trustworthy Quantum Claims

Evaluating the stability and evidential quality of reported quantum advantages and claims in published artifacts.

Reproducibility in Quantum Computing

Studying reproducibility barriers and proposing actionable protocols to strengthen empirical quantum computing research.

Empirical Quantum SE

Applying empirical software engineering methods to understand how quantum systems are built, validated, and evolved.
