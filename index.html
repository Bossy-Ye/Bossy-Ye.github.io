Research Program

Boshuai Ye

PhD Researcher in Reliable Quantum Workflows and Empirical Validation
University of Oulu

Empirical evaluation often validates outcomes, but not conclusions. I study how quantum software claims can be made trustworthy under implementation variability, execution uncertainty, and reproducibility constraints. My work combines empirical software engineering methods, validation-first workflow design, and systems-style performance modeling to make quantum software evidence more auditable, reproducible, and reliable. Current projects include ClaimStab-QC, BARC, and C2|Q>, with a broader focus on reliable quantum workflows and resource-constrained execution.

Research Themes

Claim Validation

Studying how reported conclusions in quantum software remain valid or fail under admissible implementation variability, execution uncertainty, and replication settings.

Workflow Reliability and Resource-Constrained Execution

Designing and evaluating robust hybrid workflows under scheduling, execution, and resource constraints, with connections to systems and HPC-style performance questions.

Reproducible Evidence

Building executable, auditable research artifacts with pinned environments, repeatable runs, and evidence surfaces that support independent verification and re-validation.

My research program is organized around three connected questions: when conclusions can be trusted, how workflows remain reliable under variability and resource constraints, and how evidence can be made reproducible and auditable.

Selected Contributions

  • Claim-centric validation for quantum software experiments under admissible variability.
  • Reliable hybrid quantum workflows and resource-constrained execution analysis.
  • Reproducible research artifacts and evidence surfaces for trustworthy empirical studies.

Featured Projects

ClaimStab-QC

A claim-centric validation framework for assessing whether reported quantum computing conclusions remain trustworthy under admissible implementation and execution variability.

Repository Project Website

BARC

A systems-oriented study of execution slowdown under bounded resource delivery, using structural predictors, backlog-aware metrics, and lower-bound analysis to explain when static depth misleads executable performance.

Repository

C2|Q>

A modular framework for translating classical problem specifications into executable hybrid quantum workflows, forming an early workflow foundation for later work on reliability, validation, and execution-aware analysis.

Repository ACM DOI

Quantum-Q-A-Mining

An empirical study of developer questions and knowledge gaps in quantum software, aimed at understanding recurring challenges in practice and tool support.

Repository

qRaman-Batt

An application-driven quantum workflow testbed used to study implementation reliability, evidence quality, and claim robustness in battery-related modeling tasks.
