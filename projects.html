Research Projects

These repositories are presented as research artifacts with explicit methodological purpose. The emphasis is on evidence quality, reproducibility, and engineering reliability in quantum contexts.

Program Analysis and Tooling

C2|Q>

C2|Q> is a modular classical-to-quantum framework: Python code or JSON DSL inputs are parsed into quantum-compatible formats, matched with suitable algorithms, mapped to backend options, and executed through transpilation workflows.

Why it matters: Researchers can move from classical problem statements to reproducible quantum experiments with less manual glue code and clearer evidence trails.

Funding: This project is funded by Business Finland.

Claim Stability and Reproducibility

ClaimStab-QC

Develops a systematic approach to assess how stable quantum claims remain under implementation changes, environment shifts, and alternative experimental configurations.

Why it matters: Trust in quantum progress depends on claims that remain robust beyond isolated demonstrations.

Repository Project Website

Developer Knowledge Mining

Quantum-Q-A-Mining

Mines question-answer data to analyze recurring knowledge bottlenecks, misconceptions, and practical engineering concerns in quantum software communities.

Why it matters: Empirical evidence from developer discourse helps ground research priorities in real ecosystem needs.

Domain Application Testbed

qRaman-Batt

Uses a battery-related modeling context to evaluate how quantum workflows are designed, validated, and reported in application-driven settings.

Why it matters: Application testbeds are essential for assessing practical credibility, not only algorithmic novelty.
